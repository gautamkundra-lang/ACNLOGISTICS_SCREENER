What this tool is
A daily freight-market early-warning and decision-support screener for the CPG Foods transportation & procurement team. It pulls current rates across 8 modes, scores them as headwinds or tailwinds against your network, flags escalation conditions, and turns all of it into prioritized, dollar-sized actions. Data refreshes automatically every day at noon EST. Think of it as the first thing you open with your morning coffee and the backbone of your weekly capacity-planning meeting.

Your daily routine  (≈ 5 minutes)

1 · Scan the banner & ticker

If the red escalation banner is showing at the top, a threshold has been breached — start there. Otherwise glance at the scrolling ticker for any overnight rate moves.

2 · Read the Overview

Check the CTCI trend and the Intelligence Summary. Note which modes are headwinds (red) vs tailwinds (green). This is your 30-second market read.

3 · Check Action Hub

Look at anything marked CRITICAL or HIGH. Tick off what you've done; the counter tracks your progress so nothing slips.

4 · Ask the Copilot

Unsure what a move means for your lanes? Open the Screener Copilot (bottom-right) and ask — e.g. "what changed since yesterday and what should I do?"

Your weekly routine  (planning & negotiation)

▶ Review My Network lane-by-lane

Open My Network, expand each critical lane to see its 12-week trend. Decide which lanes to lock, monitor, or play on the spot market. Export the table to CSV to share with carriers or bring into your RFP.

▶ Pressure-test the Rate Outlook

Use Rate Outlook bull/base/bear scenarios to set budget assumptions and contract-negotiation targets. Lock contracts when the bear case is steep; ride spot when the bull case dominates.

▶ Work the Action Hub by priority & deadline

Each action carries a why now, a how, a dollar impact, and a deadline. Use these to drive your weekly transportation/procurement stand-up and assign owners.

▶ Confirm escalation triggers & owners

Re-read the Escalation Triggers table. Make sure each owner (Transportation, Procurement, Planning, Customer Service) knows their threshold and the pre-agreed response before a trigger fires.

How to read the signals

▲ Headwind (red)

Rates rising against you. Consider locking capacity/contracts before they climb further.

▼ Tailwind (green)

Rates falling in your favor. A buying window — pre-book or renegotiate while it's soft.

HIGH / WATCH / EASING

Risk level. HIGH = act now, WATCH = monitor closely, EASING = pressure releasing.

Connecting your TMS or data lake

Live network lanes from your own systems
The My Network tab can be driven by your real network instead of the built-in sample lanes. Point a scheduled job in your TMS (e.g. Blue Yonder, Oracle OTM, MercuryGate, e2open) — or an ETL from your data lake (Snowflake, Databricks, BigQuery, S3) — at the ingestion endpoint below. It posts your top lanes / shipment history on whatever cadence you choose, and the dashboard renders them automatically with a LIVE source badge.
